The problem

Travel creators have the hardest monetization problem in the creator economy.

01 ·

They have the trust. Not the tools.

A travel creator with 300K followers moves more bookings than most boutique travel brands. When they say "go to Kyoto in October," their audience moves. But there's no infrastructure to capture that intent. Brand deals are the only monetization path — and brand deals commoditize their voice.

02 ·

DIY trips become a second full-time job.

Creators who try to run their own trips end up coordinating operators over WhatsApp, collecting Venmo payments, building Google Sheet rosters, and answering 400 DMs manually. It works once. No one does it twice. The best creators quietly stop trying.

03 ·

Existing tools weren't built for them.

Travel agency software is 20 years old. Airbnb Experiences is capped at $200/person. Tour operators want 200-person minimums. Nothing exists at the intersection of creator-scale reach and boutique-quality execution. That's the gap we're building in.

Why now

Three things changed
at the same time.

01 ·

Creator audiences now outperform legacy travel brands.

A travel creator with 200K engaged followers has more trusted reach than most boutique tour operators' entire customer database. The distribution already exists. It just needed infrastructure to capture it.

02 ·

AI collapsed the cost of custom curation.

Building a custom multi-day itinerary used to take a specialist hours. AI + a vetted operator knowledge base makes it 30 minutes. That's what makes the unit economics work at small group sizes. Sub-20 person trips are now viable at margin.

03 ·

No infrastructure layer exists yet.

Substack built infrastructure for writers. Beehiiv for newsletters. Shopify for products. The creator-led experience economy has no infrastructure layer. That gap is closing fast. We're building the layer before someone else does.

FAQ

Common
questions.

What size audience do I need to host a drop?
+
There's no hard follower minimum — what matters is engagement and trust. We've seen creators with 30K tight-knit followers fill a 12-person trip faster than accounts with 500K. Tell us about your audience when you apply and we'll give you an honest assessment.
What does "60% revenue share" actually mean?
+
After operator costs are paid out, you receive 60% of the net margin. We show you the full breakdown before you commit to anything — gross revenue, operator costs, our 20% platform fee, and your payout. No surprises. The Nepal example on this page is a real illustration of how the math works.
What if travelers cancel or want a refund?
+
We handle all refund requests and disputes. Our standard policy is: full refund within 30 days of booking, 50% refund up to 60 days before departure, no refund after that unless travel insurance applies. We reserve a small ops buffer per trip specifically to handle edge cases — so your payout isn't affected by individual cancellations.
Do I have to travel with the group?
+
Yes — your presence is the product. Your audience is booking an experience with you, not just a trip to Nepal. Our model is built around creators who are genuinely excited to be on the ground with their community. If you're looking for a purely passive income model, Wanderkit isn't it.
How do you handle visas, insurance, and safety?
+
Our operators are licensed and insured locally. We require all travelers to hold adequate travel + medical insurance and provide a recommended policy in the pre-trip materials. Visa requirements are clearly communicated at booking. In the event of an emergency, our operator is the first point of contact on the ground — we also maintain a 24/7 emergency line for active drops.
Can I run multiple drops per year?
+
Absolutely. Most serious creator-operators run 3–4 drops per year across different destinations. We handle each one end-to-end. Some creators use multiple drops to build an annual travel calendar for their community — and that calendar becomes a core part of their content identity.
